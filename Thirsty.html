    • Top 10 Malaysian Beverages


    1. Teh Tarik


    Teh Tarik, which translates to "pulled tea" in English, is a popular and iconic Malaysian beverage. It is made by vigorously pouring and "pulling" brewed black tea with condensed milk back and forth between two containers from a height. This process creates a frothy layer on top of the tea, resulting in a creamy and aromatic drink. The key ingredients in Teh Tarik are black tea, condensed milk, and sometimes evaporated milk or sugar, depending on personal preference. The tea is typically brewed strong and then mixed with condensed milk to add sweetness and creaminess.





    2. Sirap Bandung


    Sirap Bandung, also known simply as Bandung, is a popular and refreshing rose-flavored drink that is enjoyed in Malaysia, Singapore, and other parts of Southeast Asia. It is a sweet and creamy beverage with a distinctive pink color, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike. Sirap Bandung, rose syrup (sirap) is mixed with condensed milk and water to create a sweet and fragrant drink. The mixture is typically served over ice, resulting in a cool and refreshing beverage.





    3. Soya Cincau


    Soya Cincau, also known as "Tauhu Sumbat" or "Tau Foo Fah," is a refreshing Malaysian dessert made from soybean pudding (tau foo fah) served with grass jelly (cincau) and sweet syrup. It's a popular and nutritious dessert enjoyed by people of all ages throughout Malaysia. The Soya Cincau dessert is typically served chilled in a bowl or cup, with layers of soybean pudding and grass jelly interspersed. Sweet syrup, usually made from palm sugar or simple syrup, is drizzled over the top to add extra sweetness.





    4. Ipoh White Coffee


    Ipoh White Coffee is a famous Malaysian coffee beverage that originated from the city of Ipoh in the state of Perak, Malaysia. It is renowned for its unique roasting and preparation method, which gives the coffee its distinctive flavor and aroma. The term "white coffee" refers to the way the coffee beans are roasted with margarine or butter and sometimes sugar, resulting in a lighter roast compared to traditional dark-roasted coffee beans. The roasting process also reduces the acidity of the coffee, resulting in a smoother and milder flavor profile.





    5. Teh Air Limau Ais


    Teh Air Limau Ais is a popular Malaysian beverage that combines tea (Teh), water (Air), and lime (Limau) served over ice (Ais). It is a refreshing and tangy drink enjoyed by people of all ages throughout Malaysia, especially on hot and humid days.





    6. Air Tebu


    Air tebu, also known as sugarcane juice, is a popular and refreshing beverage in Malaysia and other parts of Southeast Asia. It is made from the juice extracted from sugarcane stalks, which are pressed through a mechanical juicer to extract the sweet liquid.





    7. Teh C Special


    Teh C Special is a popular variation of Teh C, a Malaysian beverage that combines tea (Teh) with evaporated milk (Carnation milk) and sweetened condensed milk (also known as susu manis).





    8. Air Mata Kucing


    Air Mata Kucing, which translates to "Cat's Eye Drink" in English, is a popular and refreshing herbal beverage in Malaysia. It is named for its resemblance to the eyes of a cat when the longan fruit (known as "mata kucing" in Malay) is immersed in the drink.





    9. Kopi Cham (Coffee with Tea)


    Kopi Cham or “yuen yeung” basically means “coffee with tea”. This drink gives the best of both worlds. So next time when you can’t choose between the two, order this instead! The bitterness of the coffee and the subtle fragrance of the tea contributes to the unique flavour of kopi cham. Enjoy a hot mug for a relaxing break or a chilly warm-up. Or, serve it chilled for some sweet summer refreshment.





    10. Calamansi Plum Juice with Salted Dried Plum


    Calamansi Plum Juice with Salted Dried Plum, also known as "Calamansi Asam Boi" or "Limau Kasturi Asam Boi" in Malay, is a popular Malaysian beverage that combines the tangy flavor of calamansi lime with the savory-sour taste of salted dried plum (asam boi). It's a refreshing and thirst-quenching drink enjoyed especially on hot days.
